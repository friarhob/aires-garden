## Why

The `Tags` and `Status` frontmatter fields are validated by `frontmatter_lint` (per `add-content-model`) but neither yet drives any reader-facing behaviour. Tags don't render anywhere — not on the article page, not as their own index pages — so the `Tags: rant` already on the existing `ola-jardim` post is dead metadata. The three `Status` values are accepted by the schema, but the dev/prod split for drafts is half-wired: `pelicanconf.py` doesn't expose drafts as full articles (Pelican's defaults route them to `drafts/<slug>.html`, off the homepage and lang indexes), and `publishconf.py` blanks `DRAFT_URL`/`DRAFT_SAVE_AS` without an ADR-recorded rationale. `Status: hidden` (added in ADR-0006) has no implementation at all — a hidden post would render at Pelican's default `hidden/<slug>.html`, contradicting ADR-0004's terminal-form URL rule. This change wires all three statuses and the `Tags` field into the actual rendered site.

## What Changes

- **Cross-language tag index pages at `/tag/<tag-slug>/`.** Each emits the same data shape as the homepage (translation groups, deduped by `Translation_key`), filtered to groups that include at least one article carrying the tag. Iteration unit is the group, not the article — so `/tag/rant/` shows one card for `ola-jardim` (with EN+PT badges) rather than two visible duplicates. Card links to the default-lang version if available, else the alphabetically-first translation, mirroring `/`. Generated dynamically from the union of tag slugs across all articles, so adding any tagged post auto-creates the corresponding `/tag/<slug>/` page.
- **Per-language tag index pages at `/<lang>/tag/<tag-slug>/`.** Each emits the same data shape as `/<lang>/` (per-language article list), filtered to articles in that language carrying the tag. Iteration unit is the article. Generated dynamically from the union of `(Lang, Tag-slug)` pairs across all articles, so adding a French post tagged `poesia` auto-creates `/fr/tag/poesia/`. Pelican's native cross-language tag mechanism (`TAG_URL`, `TAG_SAVE_AS`) is disabled — its iteration shape doesn't dedupe by `Translation_key`, and disabling sidesteps a competing `/tag/<slug>/` emission that would clash with ours.
- **Cross-language all-tags index at `/tags/`** and **per-language all-tags index at `/<lang>/tags/`.** The cross-language form lists every tag with at least one article (anywhere on the site); the per-language form lists every tag with at least one article in that language. Each tag links to its corresponding tag page (`/tag/<slug>/` for the cross-language index, `/<lang>/tag/<slug>/` for the per-language index). Symmetry: `/` ↔ `/tags/`, `/<lang>/` ↔ `/<lang>/tags/`.
- **Tag links rendered on each article.** Below the body, a "Tags:" line lists the article's tags, each linking to `/<lang>/tag/<tag-slug>/` (where `<lang>` is the article's own language — readers stay in-language when drilling from a post into a tag).
- **Optional authored prose on tag pages, localised on both axes.** Tag pages may carry an authored Markdown intro above the auto-generated list. The prose is a new third content type, sitting alongside `content/posts/` and `content/pages/`. The two axes are independent: the page-kind axis (cross-language `/tag/<slug>/` vs per-language `/<lang>/tag/<slug>/`) carries materially different prose because the framing differs ("all my published works" vs "all my works published in &lt;lang&gt;"), and the reader-language axis applies on the cross-language page too — a Spanish reader on `/tag/published-works/` should still see the prose in Spanish.
  - **Layout (Option I, mirrors the post directory pattern):** `content/tag-prose/<slug>/<scope>.<lang>.md`. Slug is inferred from the directory name (analogous to `Translation_key` for posts). `<scope>` is one of `all` (cross-language) or `lang` (per-language); the `all` token mirrors the scope switcher's "All" entry from i18n-rendering D6.
    ```
    content/tag-prose/published-works/
      all.en.md     → cross-lang body, EN reader on /tag/published-works/
      all.pt.md     → cross-lang body, PT reader on /tag/published-works/
      lang.en.md    → per-lang body for /en/tag/published-works/
      lang.pt.md    → per-lang body for /pt/tag/published-works/
    ```
  - **Frontmatter is a minimal third schema kind:** `Title` (required, used as the tag page heading), `Lang` (required, must equal the filename `.<lang>.` segment), `Status: hidden` (required — signals "not directly addressable" and uses the existing enum). No `Slug` (inferred from directory), no `Translation_key` (the four files of a tag are *not* a translation group; the two scopes are separate content streams, the lang variants within one scope ARE conceptually translations but don't need the cross-stream linking that posts use).
  - **All files independently optional.** Any of the four (or more, when N langs) may be present or absent. Missing combos render the corresponding tag page without prose for that (scope, reader-lang) cell.
  - **Cross-language page rendering and reader-language inference.** When 2+ `all.<lang>.md` files exist for a tag, `/tag/<slug>/` renders each as `<section data-lang="<lang>">...</section>` and embeds an inline language-inference script reused from the 404 page (per ADR-0007): stage 1 URL prefix → no match on `/tag/<slug>/` so it falls through; stage 2 `navigator.language` → match if the lang is in the site's union; fallback `<html data-default-lang>`. With JS off, all sections remain visible (graceful degradation — same bar 2 stance ADR-0007 already records). When only one `all.<lang>.md` exists, render it directly without sections or JS. When none exist, list-only.
  - **Per-language page rendering: no inference, no JS.** The URL `/<lang>/tag/<slug>/` already declares the lang; render `lang.<lang>.md`'s body if present, list-only otherwise. Single section, no fallback to `all.<lang>.md` (those are different content).
  - **Refactor (small):** the inline language-inference script currently inside `themes/garden/templates/404.html` is extracted into a Jinja partial `themes/garden/templates/_lang_inference_script.html` and `{% include %}`'d from both 404 and the cross-language tag template. Keeps ADR-0007's "inline" stance intact (Jinja includes are textual) while DRYing the duplication.
  - Markdown bodies are rendered inline by `tag_pages` via `markdown.markdown(...)` — tag-prose files don't go through Pelican's article or page generators, so there's no URL emission to suppress. `frontmatter_lint` validates the schema, the filename↔frontmatter `Lang` coupling, and the scope token at build time and via the standalone CLI.
  - **Adding tag-prose as a third content type is recorded in ADR-0009** (alongside ADR-0008 for dev-drafts), with rejected alternatives: reusing the page schema with a `Page_kind: tag-prose` marker, embedding prose in the first article tagged with the slug, and the option-II/III file-layout variants we considered.
  - **Test seed required:** `add-tags-and-drafts` cannot be verified end-to-end without at least one post tagged `published-works`, plus a `content/tag-prose/published-works/` directory containing at least `all.<lang1>.md`, `all.<lang2>.md`, and one `lang.<lang>.md` (so both the inference script and the per-lang renderer have something to exercise). See `project_published_works_seed` memory; the apply-phase tasks list will block on these before build verification.
- **Tag input strictness in `frontmatter_lint`.** Two new lint rules on the existing `Tags` field, motivated by bugs the new rendering would otherwise emit: (a) **non-empty after slugification** — a tag of `!!!` or whitespace-only slugs to `""` and would emit `/tag//`; reject; (b) **no duplicate slugs within one post's tag list** — `Tags: [Rant, rant]` slug-collide on `rant` and would render as duplicates on the article page and double-count the post in tag listings; reject. The "tags must be ≥3 chars to avoid lang collision" idea is dropped — `/tag/` and `/<lang>/` namespaces don't collide. Captured in the content-model spec as a delta; the dedupe-vs-reject choice for rule (b) is recorded in this change's `design.md`, not as an ADR.
- **Drafts visible as full articles in dev, excluded from prod.** A `dev_drafts` plugin (active when `DRAFTS_AS_PUBLISHED = True`, default in `pelicanconf.py`) promotes `Status: draft` articles into `generator.articles` / `generator.translations` before tag and lang-index generation runs, so a draft appears at `/<slug>/`, on the homepage, on its language index, and on its tag pages — exactly as it will once published. `publishconf.py` sets `DRAFTS_AS_PUBLISHED = False` and additionally blanks `DRAFT_URL`/`DRAFT_SAVE_AS`/`DRAFT_LANG_URL`/`DRAFT_LANG_SAVE_AS` so no draft file is emitted to `output/` at all.
- **`Status: hidden` articles emit at the terminal URL form.** Set `HIDDEN_ARTICLE_URL = "{slug}/"`, `HIDDEN_ARTICLE_SAVE_AS = "{slug}/index.html"`, `HIDDEN_ARTICLE_LANG_URL = "{slug}/"`, `HIDDEN_ARTICLE_LANG_SAVE_AS = "{slug}/index.html"` so a hidden post is reachable at `/<slug>/` (matching ADR-0004) but stays out of `generator.articles`, so it doesn't appear on the homepage, in language indexes, in tag pages, or in feeds. Unchanged from Pelican's default exclusion-from-listings behaviour for hidden status — only the URL form moves.
- **New `tag_pages` plugin** that consumes `i18n_grouping`'s already-computed `SITE_LANGS` and `TRANSLATION_GROUPS` context (no duplication of the grouping pass) plus its existing `_article_generator` hook pattern to emit all four tag artefacts in `finalized`: cross-language single-tag pages, per-language single-tag pages, cross-language all-tags index, per-language all-tags index. Tag slug is computed via Pelican's `slugify` so URL form matches the content-model `Slug` regex.
- **Seed content authored to exercise tag prose end-to-end.** Required additions during apply (see `project_published_works_seed`):
  - At least one post under `content/posts/` tagged `published-works` so the tag-prose pages have something to list. Ideally a real post the author plans to keep — but a thin placeholder is acceptable if no real content is ready.
  - `content/tag-prose/published-works/all.<lang1>.md` and `all.<lang2>.md` (different languages) so the cross-language inference script has 2+ sections to switch between.
  - At least one `content/tag-prose/published-works/lang.<lang>.md` so the per-language renderer is exercised.
  - The existing `ola-jardim` post (`Tags: rant`, EN+PT) covers the no-prose path (`/tag/rant/` and `/en/tag/rant/` render list-only) and the tag-link rendering on the article body.
  - A draft isn't authored as part of this change; the `dev_drafts` plugin is verified by temporarily flipping the `Status` of a sibling test post during build verification (and reverted before commit).
- **Record ADR-0008** documenting the dev-promotes-drafts stance and why `DRAFTS_AS_PUBLISHED` is the toggle (rather than e.g. an env var, a separate dev plugin, or duplicating template logic).

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `tags-and-drafts`: cross-language and per-language tag index pages (deduped-by-`Translation_key` for the cross-language form), cross-language and per-language all-tags indexes, tag links on articles, drafts visible as full articles in dev and absent in prod, hidden articles at terminal URLs, native Pelican tag mechanism disabled.

### Modified Capabilities
- `content-model`:
  - The `Tags is a list of non-empty strings` requirement gains two new scenarios — "tag slugifies to non-empty" and "no duplicate tag slugs within one post." The lint behaviour is real new contract, not implementation drift, so it lives as a delta on the existing requirement.
  - A new requirement, `Tag-prose files have a schema-validated frontmatter`, is added alongside the existing post-schema and page-schema requirements. Defines required/forbidden fields for the third content type, plus filename↔frontmatter coupling rules analogous to (but smaller than) the post rules. `frontmatter_lint`'s schema module gains a `parse_tag_prose_frontmatter` (or equivalent) entry and the standalone CLI traverses `content/tag-prose/` alongside `content/posts/` and `content/pages/`.

## Impact

- **New plugins:**
  - `plugins/tag_pages/` — emits all four tag artefacts (cross-language single-tag, per-language single-tag, cross-language all-tags index, per-language all-tags index) and discovers + renders authored tag prose. Consumes `i18n_grouping`'s `SITE_LANGS` and `TRANSLATION_GROUPS` from `generator.context` (no re-grouping). Renders cross-language single-tag via `tag_group_index.html` (group iteration, like `index.html`), per-language single-tag via `tag_lang_index.html` (article iteration, like `lang_index.html`), all-tags indexes via `tags_list.html`. Tag-prose discovery walks `content/tag-prose/<slug>/`, picks up files matching `<scope>.<lang>.md` where `<scope> ∈ {all, lang}`, parses each via the existing `plugins.frontmatter_lint.schema` (new tag-prose entrypoint), and renders Markdown bodies via `markdown.markdown(...)` directly. The plugin builds two dicts per tag — `all_prose: dict[lang, html]` and `lang_prose: dict[lang, html]` — and passes them into the matching templates: cross-language single-tag template receives `all_prose` (rendering one section per lang plus the inference script when len ≥ 2, or one block directly when len == 1, or nothing when len == 0); per-language single-tag template receives `lang_prose.get(page_lang)` as `tag_prose_html`. Tag slug is computed via Pelican's `slugify` so URL form matches the content-model `Slug` regex.
  - `plugins/dev_drafts/` — when `DRAFTS_AS_PUBLISHED` is truthy in settings, promotes everything in `generator.drafts + generator.drafts_translations` to `generator.articles + generator.translations` (mutates each article's `status` to `published`) before `article_generator_finalized` returns. Connected with high signal priority so it runs before `i18n_grouping`'s grouping pass — keeps draft articles inside the translation groups they belong to. No-op when the setting is False or absent.
  - Both registered in `pelicanconf.py`'s `PLUGINS` alongside the existing three. Kept separate per the same lifecycle/consumer split that justified the i18n-rendering two-plugin choice (D1).
- **`frontmatter_lint` schema module** (`plugins/frontmatter_lint/schema.py`) gains:
  - Two checks on the existing `Tags` field: each tag must produce a non-empty slug under Pelican's `slugify`, and no two tags within one post may produce the same slug. Failures use the same file-anchored, grouped error format already specified in the content-model spec.
  - A new tag-prose schema entrypoint validating `content/tag-prose/` files: required `Title`, `Lang`, `Status` (with `Status` constrained to `hidden`); forbidden `Slug`, `Translation_key`, `Tags`; filename↔frontmatter coupling for the `.lang.` segment when present.
  - CLI (`python -m frontmatter_lint`) inherits the new checks for free and grows a third traversal target (`content/tag-prose/`) alongside `content/posts/` and `content/pages/`.
- **Templates:**
  - New: `themes/garden/templates/tag_group_index.html` (cross-language single-tag, group-iterated, mirrors `index.html`; renders 0/1/N `<section data-lang>` prose blocks above the list, with the inference script included when N ≥ 2), `themes/garden/templates/tag_lang_index.html` (per-language single-tag, article-iterated, mirrors `lang_index.html`; renders single prose block above the list when present, list-only otherwise), `themes/garden/templates/tags_list.html` (all-tags index, used for both `/tags/` and `/<lang>/tags/`; the `page_lang` variable, when set, scopes the listing).
  - New: `themes/garden/templates/_lang_inference_script.html` — Jinja partial extracted from the inline `<script>` currently embedded in `404.html`. Pure refactor: same script, two `{% include %}` consumers (the existing `404.html` and the new `tag_group_index.html`).
  - Edit: `themes/garden/templates/404.html` to `{% include "_lang_inference_script.html" %}` instead of carrying the inline script verbatim. No behaviour change for the 404 page; verification covers it.
  - Edit: `themes/garden/templates/article.html` to add the "Tags:" line below the body, hidden when `article.tags` is empty. Each tag links to `/<article.lang>/tag/<tag-slug>/`.
  - Edit: `themes/garden/templates/base.html` — no scope-switcher change (the switcher's contract from i18n-rendering already handles tag-page contexts: cross-language tag pages behave like `/`, per-language tag pages behave like `/<lang>/`). Verified, not edited.
- **Config:**
  - `pelicanconf.py` adds `dev_drafts` and `tag_pages` to `PLUGINS`, sets `DRAFTS_AS_PUBLISHED = True`, sets `HIDDEN_ARTICLE_URL`/`HIDDEN_ARTICLE_SAVE_AS`/`HIDDEN_ARTICLE_LANG_URL`/`HIDDEN_ARTICLE_LANG_SAVE_AS` to the terminal `{slug}/` form, and disables Pelican's native tag mechanism with `TAG_URL = ""` / `TAG_SAVE_AS = ""` / `TAGS_URL = ""` / `TAGS_SAVE_AS = ""`.
  - `publishconf.py` overrides `DRAFTS_AS_PUBLISHED = False` and keeps the existing four `DRAFT_*` blanks (now with explicit rationale recorded in ADR-0008).
- **No new dependencies.** Uses Pelican's `slugify` (already on the import path via `pelican.utils`), the existing `i18n_grouping` context, and standard Jinja2 templating.
- **URL surface added (verification target):**
  - `/tag/rant/` and `/tag/published-works/` — cross-language, deduped, list-only for `rant` (no prose) and prose-decorated for `published-works` (per the seed content).
  - `/en/tag/rant/`, `/pt/tag/rant/`, `/en/tag/published-works/`, `/pt/tag/published-works/` — per-language, article-iterated; `published-works` variants render the matching `lang.<lang>.md` prose above the list.
  - `/tags/` — cross-language all-tags index (entries: `rant`, `published-works`).
  - `/en/tags/`, `/pt/tags/` — per-language all-tags indexes.
  - No URL break for existing pages.
- **Decision log:** two new ADRs committed alongside this bundle and indexed in `openspec/decisions/README.md`:
  - **ADR-0008** (`dev-drafts-promotion.md`) — toggle-driven dev-only promotion of drafts to articles, rationale for using a `DRAFTS_AS_PUBLISHED` setting over an env var or a separate dev-only plugin.
  - **ADR-0009** (`tag-prose-content-type.md`) — introducing tag-prose as a third content type alongside posts and pages, with the rejected alternatives (page-schema with `Page_kind: tag-prose` marker, prose embedded in the first article carrying the tag, file-layout options II and III). Establishes the directory-anchored content-type pattern that future content kinds (notes, snippets, etc.) can reference.
  - No ADR for the tag-input-strictness rules (rationale lives in this change's `design.md`) — those tighten an existing field, they don't introduce a project-level pattern.
- **Future changes unblocked:**
  - `add-design-tokens` (gallery cards can now surface tag chips with real tag-page targets).
  - `add-python-cli` (`garden new` defaults `Status: draft`; this change ensures dev workflow renders that draft as a full article immediately, closing the author's preview loop. A `garden tag-prose <slug> <scope> <lang>` scaffold subcommand becomes a natural addition once the content type is established).
